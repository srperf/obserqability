# Enter obserQAbility !!
This repository will give you an introduction into the principles of obserQAbility, together with some examples.

## What is observability (o11y)?
Observability is to be able to know the internal state of something through information available externally.

In other words, to know how is something without lifting the lid.

## What do I need to achieve o11y?
There are some requirements you must fulfill if you want to have o11y:

- Telemetry: The capacity to transfer information from the insides of your software. This is achieved through the second requirement.
- Instrumentation: Is the insertion of code, libraries or devices that enable telemetry and transmit elsewhere the information of interest. This could be done manually, or through Client Libraries, or through agents.
- Storage: If you need to analyze past information about your software, you need somewhere to store that information. Usually time based databases, but any storage can work.
- Visualizations: Data generated by o11y often comes in formats that humans cannot understand. You need visualization platforms to display and refresh the data in pretty and easy to understand images. 

# What is ObserQAbility
ObserQAbility (I would like to short it as oQAy, pronounced as Oh-kiu-ey-ee) is the fusion of two practices, QA and Observability. Which means integrating these practices both ways, o11y techniques inside of QA practices, and QA techniques integrated inside o11y.
